Family Composition

Understanding the multi-generational travel dynamic

Parents (Decision Makers)

Age: 30-45 years old (Post-80s/90s)

Role: Primary decision-makers and planners

Characteristics: Tech-savvy, well-educated, value both luxury and meaningful experiences

Children

Age: 6-12 years old (School-age)

Role: Educational focus and family bonding

Characteristics: Curious, tech-native, seek fun learning experiences

Grandparents

Age: 60-75 years old

Role: Cultural transmission and family continuity

Characteristics: Healthy, active, value comfort and safety

Three generations sharing experiences and creating lasting memories together

Spending Power & Preferences

Premium experiences for discerning families

Financial Profile

  • Household Income: ≥ ¥800,000 RMB annually
  • Travel Budget: High daily spending capacity
  • Willingness to Pay: Premium for customization and quality
  • Spending Pattern: Value-driven, not price-sensitive

Experience Preferences

  • Edutainment Focus: Blend of education and entertainment
  • Family Bonding: Activities that connect generations
  • Safety & Comfort: Non-negotiable requirements
  • Shareability: Instagrammable, memorable moments

Affluent families allocate significantly more budget to premium experiences vs. material goods

Psychology & Behavior

What drives their travel decisions

Edutainment Seekers

They prioritize experiences that combine learning with entertainment, valuing both children's education and family fun equally.

Family Connection Focus

Travel is seen as an opportunity to strengthen family bonds across generations and create shared memories.

Digital Natives

Heavily influenced by social media (Xiaohongshu, Douyin) and authentic KOL recommendations for travel inspiration.

Xiaohongshu and Douyin are primary sources for travel inspiration and planning

Decision-Making Factors

What matters most in their travel choices

1

Safety & Security

Non-negotiable requirement, especially when traveling with children and elderly family members.

2

Quality of Experience

Willing to pay premium for well-curated, authentic, and meaningful experiences.

3

Family-Friendliness

Activities and accommodations must cater to all age groups simultaneously.

4

Social Proof

Heavily influenced by reviews, recommendations, and social media content.
